# Trading Guide

## Order Types

### Market Order
Execute immediately at the best available price.

**Best for**: Quick execution when price is less important than speed.

### Limit Order
Set a specific price. Order executes only when market reaches your price.

**Best for**: Price-sensitive trades where you want control.

### Stop Order
Triggered when price reaches a specific level, converts to market order.

**Best for**: Risk management and automated trading strategies.

## Order Book

The order book shows:
- **Bids**: Buy orders (green)
- **Asks**: Sell orders (red)
- **Spread**: Difference between best bid and ask

## Chart Reading

### Candlesticks
- **Green**: Price went up
- **Red**: Price went down
- **Body**: Opening to closing price
- **Wicks**: High and low prices

### Indicators
- **SMA**: Simple Moving Average - trend indicator
- **RSI**: Relative Strength Index - momentum indicator
- **Volume**: Trading activity indicator

## Risk Management

1. **Position Sizing**: Never risk more than 1-2% per trade
2. **Stop Loss**: Always set stop-loss orders
3. **Diversification**: Don't concentrate positions
4. **Risk/Reward**: Aim for at least 2:1 risk/reward ratio

## Common Mistakes

1. **FOMO Trading**: Don't chase price movements
2. **Overtrading**: Quality over quantity
3. **Ignoring Stop Loss**: Always use stop-loss
4. **Emotional Trading**: Stick to your strategy
